## __what i'm reading__
### *Apocalyptic Fever: End-Time Prophecies in Modern America* and *Forest Euphoria: The Abounding Queerness of Nature*

_Forest Euphoria_ makes me want to go camping. I'm envious of naturalists and biologists who can go out into the world and whip out the binomial nomenclature of plants and animals. The world is more colorful and alive when you can acknowledge the different forms of life all around you.

In contrast, learning about dispensational premillennialism from _Apocalyptic Fever_ makes the West's unwavering death-march into climate collapse make sense. If the _de facto_ evangelical Christian leadership of the world believe that the apocalypse is coming, and they're getting gracefully raptured before the deadliest events of history prepare the way for a thousand years of Utopia, no wonder they don't give a shit about anything or anyone. 

The AI 'effective altruism' cult shares a lot with premillennialism: at some indeterminate point, the arrival of an omnipotent and wrathful God ushers  in Utopia for the elect and eradication for the non-believers (Roko's Basilisk). The 'infinite benefit' to future generations of AGI thus justifies crimes the elect's crimes against everyone else, whether it's Silicon Valley's collaboration with the fascist war machine and surveillance state, Sam Bankman-Fried's ponzi scheme, the Zizians' literal murders, or Sam Altman in general.

## __something i hated__
### the consequences of not backing up your shit
I run every single one of my self-hosted services from a single Docker compose file. Then I extracted another compose file right on top of it, irrevocably destroying it. I spent hours re-writing it again by hand.

I learned nothing from the experience and ended up destroying the compose file again. Sufficiently punished, I'm now working on setting up [borg](https://www.borgbackup.org/) and [borgmatic](https://torsion.org/borgmatic/) on all my devices.
